## How to Negotiate Salary

### Research Your Market Value
1. Check [Levels.fyi](https://levels.fyi) for tech company data points
2. Use [Glassdoor](https://glassdoor.com), [LinkedIn Salary](https://linkedin.com/salary), [Payscale](https://payscale.com)
3. Ask peers in communities (Blind, Discord servers, Reddit r/cscareerquestions)
4. Talk to recruiters — they know market rates
5. Get multiple offers simultaneously for the strongest leverage

### Negotiation Principles
- **Always negotiate** — 80%+ of offers have room
- **Anchor high** — your first number should be at or above your target
- **Negotiate total comp**, not just base (equity, bonus, signing, PTO)
- **Use competing offers** as the most powerful leverage
- **Counter in writing** for a paper trail and time to think
- **Never name a number first** — let them anchor

### Questions to Ask During Offer
- What is the equity refresh schedule?
- When was the last 409A valuation and what was the strike price?
- What does the typical bonus payout look like historically?
- Is there a signing bonus, and is it clawback within 12 months?
- How does performance review affect equity grants?

## Equity 101 (For Startup Offers)

| Term | What It Means |
|------|--------------|
| **RSU** (Restricted Stock Unit) | Stock granted to you, vests over time — common at public companies |
| **ISO/NSO Stock Options** | Right to *buy* stock at strike price — common at startups |
| **Vesting Schedule** | Typically 4 years with 1-year cliff (25% at 1 year, then monthly) |
| **409A Valuation** | Independent appraisal of company's fair market value |
| **Strike Price** | Price you pay to exercise options (ideally much less than FMV) |
| **Liquidation Preference** | VCs get paid first in acquisition — affects your payout |
| **Option Pool** | Reserved equity for employees — dilutes all holders when expanded |
| **Secondary Market** | Platforms to sell private stock before IPO (Forge, Nasdaq Private Market) |

> **Key question for startups:** "What is your company's last valuation, and what percentage of the company does my equity represent?"
