# Networking Guide for DS / MLE / AIE Roles

> 70-80% of jobs are filled through networking. This isn't just about who you know — it's about being visible, helpful, and building genuine professional relationships.

---

## Why Networking Matters in AI/ML

The AI/ML field is small and moves fast:
- Many positions are filled before being posted
- Referrals bypass ATS filters entirely (critical for ML roles)
- Research papers, blog posts, and open-source work are the best resume
- Technical communities (Discords, Twitter/X, GitHub) are where opportunities appear first

### LinkedIn — Professional Visibility

**Profile optimization for ML/AI:**

1. **Headline** — Not just "Data Scientist at Acme"
   - Better: "ML Engineer | Building production AI systems | Python · PyTorch · LLMs"

2. **About section** — 3 paragraphs:
   - Paragraph 1: What you do and who you help
   - Paragraph 2: What you're most proud of (specific project)
   - Paragraph 3: What you're looking for / interested in

3. **Experience bullets** — Same as resume impact bullets

4. **Featured section** — Pin your best GitHub project, blog post, or demo

5. **Skills section** — Add specific tools, not just "Machine Learning"
   - Add: PyTorch, LangChain, MLflow, FastAPI, XGBoost, RAGAS, etc.

**LinkedIn posting strategy:**
- Post 1x/week about something you learned or built
- Comment thoughtfully on posts by people you want to connect with
- Share your projects with brief context: "Shipped X. Here's what I learned."
- Engage with Yann LeCun, Andrew Ng, Andrej Karpathy, etc. — their followers are your target audience

## Building in Public Strategy

The most powerful networking is showing your work:

**Week 1-4 plan:**
```
Week 1: Share your learning goal publicly
  → "Starting to learn RAG this month. Will share weekly updates."

Week 2: Share a specific technical insight
  → "Learned: chunk_overlap of 200 chars significantly improves RAGAS
     context_recall from 0.72 to 0.88. Here's why..."

Week 3: Share a mini-project with code
  → "Built a 50-line RAG pipeline with RAGAS evaluation.
     GitHub: [link] Key takeaway: [insight]"

Week 4: Share final project + metrics
  → "Month 1 complete. Built doc Q&A with:
     - 0.89 faithfulness
     - Hybrid BM25+semantic search
     - Deployed on HuggingFace Spaces
     Live demo: [link] Code: [link]"
```

**Why this works:**
- Demonstrates competence (better than just claiming it)
- Attracts recruiters and engineers who follow specific topics
- Creates artifacts interviewers actually review
- Gets you feedback from practitioners

## Informational Interviews

The single most underused career accelerator.

**What is it:** A 20-30 minute conversation with someone in a role you want, to learn about their experience — NOT to ask for a job.

### How to request:

```
Subject: Quick question about your work at [Company]

Hi [Name],

I've been following your work on [specific thing they've written/built].
I'm transitioning from [current role] into [target role] and would love
to learn from someone who's done it.

Would you be open to a 20-minute Zoom call sometime in the next few weeks?
I have specific questions about [X, Y, Z] and would be happy to work around
your schedule.

No worries at all if you're too busy!

Best,
[Your name]
[LinkedIn] [GitHub]
```

**What to ask in the call:**

```
✓ How did you transition into [role]?
✓ What does a typical day look like for you?
✓ What skills were most important that you didn't expect?
✓ What would you do differently if starting over?
✓ Who else would you recommend I talk to?
✓ Is there anything you'd recommend I work on specifically?
```

**Closing:**
```
"This was incredibly helpful. Is there anything I can do for you in return?
I'm happy to share resources, review code, or make introductions."
```

**After the call:**
- Send a thank you email within 24 hours
- Connect on LinkedIn with a note referencing what you discussed
- Follow up in 2-3 months to share your progress

### Response rates:
- Cold LinkedIn DM: ~5-10% response rate
- Cold email (if you can find it): ~10-20%
- With a mutual connection: ~50-70%
- After engaging with their content: ~20-30%

## Job Search Strategy

### Active Job Search (applying)

**Where to look:**
- `linkedin.com/jobs` — Best for company search, network view
- `lever.co` / `greenhouse.io` — Common ATS systems (apply directly)
- `wellfound.com` (AngelList) — Startups, early-stage
- `jobs.ashbyhq.com` — Many AI/ML startups
- Company career pages directly — Anthropic, OpenAI, Cohere, Hugging Face
- Kaggle Jobs — DS-specific listings

### Passive Job Search (letting opportunities come to you)

**Setup and forget:**
- LinkedIn "Open to Work" (visible only to recruiters)
- Update LinkedIn headline with your specialization
- Star relevant GitHub repos to signal interests to recruiters
- Publish blog posts/projects regularly

**Response to recruiter outreach:**
```
"Thanks for reaching out. I'm selectively open to opportunities.
What I'm looking for:
- [Role type]
- [Company stage/size preference]
- [Compensation range] total comp
- [Location/remote requirements]

Could you share more about the role and team?
I'm happy to have a quick call if it seems like a fit."
```

## Referral Strategy

Referrals are 4-6x more likely to convert to an offer than cold applications.

**How to get referrals:**

1. **Build genuine relationships first** (not transactional "will you refer me?")

2. **Engage before asking:**
   - Comment on their posts
   - Contribute to their open-source projects
   - Share their work with attribution

3. **Make it easy to say yes:**
   ```
   "I'm applying for the ML Engineer role at [Company] — the team
   working on [specific project] looks exciting. Would you be willing
   to put in a referral? I'm happy to send you my resume and a
   brief note about why I'm a fit. No pressure if it's not a good time."
   ```

4. **Reciprocate:** Always ask how you can help them in return.

## 30-Day Networking Action Plan

| Week | Actions |
|------|---------|
| **Week 1** | Polish LinkedIn + GitHub. Write 1 post about your work. |
| **Week 2** | Send 5 informational interview requests. Comment on 10 posts. |
| **Week 3** | Attend 1 virtual event or meetup. Follow up with people met. |
| **Week 4** | Share a project publicly. Ask 2 people for referrals if applying. |

- 2-3 informational interviews booked
- 5+ meaningful LinkedIn connections with context
- 1 piece of public content posted
- Joined at least 2 relevant Discord/Slack communities
