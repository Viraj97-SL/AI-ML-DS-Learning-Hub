### Generative AI & Foundation Models

#### Overview

Generative AI is a branch of artificial intelligence focused on creating new, original content—from text and images to music and code. Instead of just analyzing or classifying data, these models *generate* it. The engine behind many of these powerful tools is a **Foundation Model**, which is a large-scale model pre-trained on a vast quantity of data. These models, like GPT or Stable Diffusion, can be adapted for a wide range of downstream tasks such as question-answering, text summarization, and image generation, making them a cornerstone of modern AI development.

#### Projects & Exercises

1.  **Build a Persona Chatbot:** Use a foundation model API to create a simple chatbot. Your goal is to use prompt engineering to give it a specific persona (e.g., a helpful librarian, a sarcastic robot, or a Shakespearean poet).
2.  **Automated Text Summarizer:** Write a script that takes a long article URL as input, scrapes its text, and uses an LLM to generate a concise, one-paragraph summary.
3.  **Explore Different Models:** Choose a simple prompt (e.g., "write a short story about a robot who discovers gardening"). Run this prompt through at least three different publicly available models and compare their outputs in terms of creativity, coherence, and style.
