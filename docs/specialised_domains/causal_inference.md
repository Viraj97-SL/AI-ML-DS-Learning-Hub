### Specialised Domains

#### Overview

Once you have a solid grasp of the fundamental concepts in data science, the next step to elevate your career is to develop domain expertise. A generalist can build a model, but a specialist understands the nuances of the data, asks the right questions, and translates results into actionable insights for a specific field like finance, healthcare, or e-commerce. Specializing allows you to tackle more complex, meaningful problems and deliver significantly more value. For example, instead of just knowing how to build a language model, a specialist might fine-tune an LLM on medical research papers to create a tool that helps doctors find relevant information faster.

#### Projects/Exercises

1.  **Domain-Specific EDA:** Choose a domain you're passionate about (e.g., sports analytics, climate science, video games). Find a relevant dataset on a platform like Kaggle. Perform an exploratory data analysis (EDA) and write a report summarizing three key insights that would be valuable to an expert in that field.
2.  **Specialized Model Application:** Pick a common business problem within a specific industry (e.g., predicting customer churn for a telecom company, detecting fraud in financial transactions). Research the common metrics and challenges associated with that problem, then build and evaluate a model tailored to that specific context.
